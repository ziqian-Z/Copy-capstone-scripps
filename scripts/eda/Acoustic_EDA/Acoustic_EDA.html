EDA For the merged_all_201203 dataset

This heatmap shows where the water is warmer and colder. The numbers inside each box show how many whale calls were detected in that area. Whales seem to prefer cooler waters and might avoid warmer areas. Temperature may be one reason they choose where to go!

Salinity does not show a strong correlation with whale call density.

Whales prefer deep, low-pressure areas, while shallow, high-pressure areas have fewer detections. This matches what we expect since many whales live and communicate in deep waters!

This map shows how ocean currents move north and south in different areas. Red means the water is moving more northward, while blue means it's moving more southward. The boxes show where whales were detected, possibly linking whale activity to these water movements.

This map shows how ocean currents move east and west in different areas. Red means the water is moving more eastward, while blue means it's moving more westward. The boxes show where whales were detected, which might help understand how whales interact with these ocean currents.

Whales are present in areas with both eastward and westward currents, so zonal velocity might not be a major factor in their movement or calling behavior. However, it could still influence their travel paths or food availability.

Depth and pressure show a strong correlation with whale detections, meaning whales favor deep sea environments.

Temperature and salinity do not show strong trends, implying that they do not significantly impact whale call activity.
